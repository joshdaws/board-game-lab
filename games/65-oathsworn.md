## Design Notes

Designer: Jamie Jolly. Published by Shadowborne Games (2022).

### Theme Integration

Oathsworn plunges players into the Deepwood, a dark fantasy wilderness where
humanity clings to the last Free Company -- a mercenary band sworn to protect
a desperate settlement from the horrors lurking beyond the treeline. The tone
is not heroic fantasy; it is survival horror filtered through a grimdark lens.
The Deepwood is not an environment to explore -- it is an environment that
hunts you. Every encounter is framed as a confrontation with something ancient,
terrible, and far more powerful than the players. The narrative chapters
between battles establish atmosphere through choose-your-own-adventure
sequences that emphasize vulnerability, moral ambiguity, and consequence.
Characters make decisions with imperfect information, and the Deepwood
punishes confidence as readily as it punishes cowardice.

The Oathsworn themselves -- the player characters -- are defined by their
martial identities. Each class represents a specific combat philosophy:
the Ranger operates at distance, the Warden absorbs punishment, the Blade
excels at precise strikes. But their narrative identities are equally
important. Between battles, characters participate in story chapters where
their choices affect morale, resources, and the branching campaign path.
The game treats its characters as people with fears and obligations, not
just combat stat blocks. This dual identity -- fighter and person -- is
central to the game's emotional register. You care about your characters
because the story gives them context, and you fight harder in battles
because the narrative has established stakes.

The boss-centric design philosophy deserves special attention. Oathsworn
does not feature waves of generic enemies or dungeon corridors full of
interchangeable monsters. Every combat encounter is a boss fight -- a
singular, massive creature with unique attack patterns, behavioral phases,
and thematic personality. A colossal spider does not fight like a corrupted
tree-spirit, and neither fights like a burrowing horror. Each boss is a
puzzle to be decoded under pressure, and the physical presence of the
oversized miniatures or standees dominates the table, making every
encounter feel like an event rather than a routine skirmish. The design
declares that fewer, more meaningful encounters are better than numerous
forgettable ones.

### Core Loop

Each chapter of the campaign alternates between two distinct phases:

**STORY PHASE**
1. NARRATIVE ENCOUNTER - Read a story chapter from the campaign book (or
   use the companion app for voice-acted narration). Chapters present
   branching scenarios with multiple decision points.
2. CHOOSE YOUR PATH - At decision points, players collectively choose
   how to proceed. Choices may involve diplomacy, investigation,
   resource management, or moral dilemmas. Some choices are voted on;
   others are assigned to a specific character.
3. CONSEQUENCE - Decisions affect the campaign state: morale levels,
   available resources, NPC relationships, and which boss encounter
   awaits. Some consequences are immediate; others ripple across
   multiple chapters.

**BATTLE PHASE**
1. SETUP - Place the boss miniature/standee and terrain on the battle
   map. Each player selects their character's starting equipment and
   ability cards. The boss's behavior deck is shuffled.
2. PLAYER TURNS - Each Oathsworn takes a turn consisting of:
   - MOVE (spend movement points to reposition on the grid)
   - PLAY CARDS (spend battleflow cards from hand for attacks, defenses,
     or special abilities; card strength scales with the number of
     matching symbols in the played set)
   - EMPOWER or REST (empower to boost a future action by adding cards
     to the empower pool; rest to recover spent cards from discard back
     to hand)
3. BOSS TURN - Draw and resolve a boss behavior card. The boss acts
   according to its behavioral rules: targeting priorities, movement
   patterns, and attack zones. Boss attacks target specific grid areas
   and deal damage based on the encounter's difficulty.
4. MIGHTY DEED CHECK - When a player achieves a critical combination
   of played cards (specific symbol thresholds), they trigger a mighty
   deed -- a powerful heroic action that can deal massive damage, heal
   allies, or alter the battlefield.
5. BOSS PHASE TRANSITION - When the boss takes enough damage, it enters
   a new behavioral phase with altered attack patterns, new abilities,
   and increased aggression. Boss fights typically have 2-3 phases.
6. RESOLUTION - Victory if the boss is defeated. Failure if all
   Oathsworn are incapacitated. Campaign continues either way, but
   failure carries narrative and mechanical consequences.

### Key Design Patterns

BATTLEFLOW CARD SYSTEM: Oathsworn replaces traditional dice-based combat
with a card-driven system called Battleflow. Each character has a personal
deck of Battleflow cards marked with colored symbols (red for attack, blue
for defense, yellow for special abilities, white as wild). To perform an
action, you play cards from hand and count matching symbols -- more matches
mean more powerful results. A 3-red-symbol attack does significantly more
damage than a 1-red-symbol attack. The system preserves the variance that
makes combat exciting (you draw from a deck, so outcomes are uncertain)
while giving players meaningful agency over their probability distribution
(you choose which cards to play and when to empower). This is a superior
alternative to pure dice rolling because it creates hand-management
decisions layered onto the combat system. Every card played is a card
not available for later turns, creating a resource-depletion arc across
the battle. The design insight: replacing dice with cards transforms
combat from reaction (roll and respond) to planning (manage hand across
multiple turns).

EMPOWER/REST CYCLE: The empower/rest system is the action economy's
heartbeat. Empowering moves cards from your hand to a separate empower
pool, where they augment your next action -- the empowered action adds
the pooled cards' symbols to the played cards' symbols, enabling much
more powerful attacks or defenses. Resting retrieves spent cards from
your discard pile back to hand, refreshing your options. The tension is
immediate: empowering now makes your next action explosive but leaves
you with fewer cards for emergency defense. Resting refills your hand
but sacrifices your turn's offensive output. This push-pull rhythm
creates the battle's pacing -- players oscillate between building
toward powerful turns and recovering from resource expenditure. The
design pattern generalizes: a two-phase action economy (charge/release)
creates natural dramatic oscillation within a longer encounter without
requiring explicit round-counting or phase triggers.

BOSS-FIGHT FOCUSED DESIGN: Every combat encounter in Oathsworn is a
boss fight. There are no trash mobs, no filler encounters, no
experience-grinding skirmishes. Every battle pits the party against a
single massive creature (or occasionally a primary boss with minor
supporting elements). This design commitment has cascading effects:
each encounter can be meticulously balanced and playtested because there
are fewer of them; each boss can have unique mechanics, behavioral
phases, and tactical puzzles because the design budget is concentrated;
and players approach every battle with heightened investment because
there are no throwaway fights. The trade-off is reduced encounter
variety within a session, but the campaign's chapter structure
compensates by spacing battles between substantial narrative segments.
The design lesson: fewer, deeper encounters create more memorable
experiences than numerous shallow ones, provided the pacing framework
(story chapters) prevents monotony.

MIGHTY DEED HEROIC MOMENTS: When a player achieves a critical mass of
specific card symbols in a single action (the threshold varies by
character class and equipment), they trigger a mighty deed -- a
dramatically powerful ability that transcends normal action limits.
Mighty deeds can deal devastating damage, heal the entire party, or
reposition the boss. They are designed to be rare, climactic moments
that feel earned rather than routine. The mechanical function is peak
creation: mighty deeds give players a goal to build toward across
multiple turns (accumulating the right cards through empowering) and
provide a cathartic payoff that punctuates the battle's rhythm. The
design principle: systems that create occasional extraordinary moments
-- achievable through skilled play but not guaranteed -- generate more
memorable experiences than systems that deliver consistent moderate
outcomes.

NARRATIVE CONSEQUENCE TRACKING: Campaign decisions in story chapters
produce consequences that persist across the campaign. Choices about
which NPCs to trust, which paths to investigate, and which moral
compromises to accept alter the available encounters, resource levels,
and story branches in subsequent chapters. The consequence system
operates on multiple timescales: some decisions have immediate effects
(gaining or losing supplies before a battle), others alter the
mid-campaign trajectory (which boss you face next), and some only
resolve in the campaign's final chapters. This multi-layered
consequence tracking gives weight to narrative decisions because
players cannot fully predict when or how their choices will matter.
The design insight: consequences that resolve at unpredictable future
points create more tension than immediate cause-and-effect, because
uncertainty about WHEN a decision matters makes every decision feel
potentially significant.

DEEPWOOD AS OPPRESSIVE ATMOSPHERE: The Deepwood setting is not a
backdrop -- it is an active antagonist. The narrative consistently
frames the forest as malevolent, ancient, and incomprehensible. Sound
design (via the companion app), illustration, and prose all reinforce
a claustrophobic hostility. This atmospheric commitment serves a
mechanical function: it establishes the emotional register in which
all decisions are made. Players approach choices defensively rather
than adventurously because the world has taught them that boldness is
punished and caution is rewarded (but never guaranteed to succeed).
The atmosphere makes the boss fights feel like desperate last stands
rather than voluntary confrontations. The pattern: consistent tonal
commitment across all game channels (narrative, art, music, mechanics)
creates emotional coherence that amplifies mechanical tension. A boss
fight in a lighthearted setting feels different from the same mechanical
encounter in a horror setting, even if the rules are identical.

CHOOSE-YOUR-OWN-ADVENTURE BETWEEN BATTLES: Between boss fights, the
game shifts entirely to narrative mode. Players read (or listen to)
branching story chapters, make collective decisions, and experience
the consequences of previous choices. This structural alternation
serves multiple functions: it provides pacing relief after intense
tactical combat; it builds emotional investment in characters and
setting that makes the next battle more meaningful; and it creates
campaign-level strategic decisions (resource allocation, path
selection) that exist on a different decision axis from the tactical
combat. The branching narrative also provides significant replayability
-- different story paths lead to different bosses, different
resources, and different emotional arcs. The design principle:
alternating between fundamentally different game modes (tactical
combat vs. narrative choice) prevents fatigue in either mode and
creates a rhythm that sustains engagement across a long campaign.

BOSS BEHAVIORAL PHASES: Each boss transitions through distinct
behavioral phases as it takes damage, fundamentally changing its
attack patterns and capabilities mid-fight. A boss in phase 1
might use sweeping area attacks; in phase 2, it becomes more mobile
and targets isolated characters; in phase 3, it enters a desperate
frenzy with higher damage and new abilities. Phase transitions are
the battle's structural turning points, forcing players to adapt
their tactics in real time. The multi-phase design creates a
natural dramatic arc within each encounter (introduction, escalation,
climax) and prevents the common boss-fight problem of
front-loading difficulty (where the boss is hardest at the start
and easiest when nearly dead). Instead, bosses become more
dangerous as they take damage, maintaining tension through the
entire encounter.

### What Makes It #65

Oathsworn's contribution to cooperative campaign gaming is its
insistence on quality over quantity in every dimension. Every battle
is a boss fight, meticulously designed with unique mechanics and
behavioral phases. Every narrative chapter is a branching story with
meaningful consequences. The Battleflow card system replaces dice
with genuine hand-management decisions. And the empower/rest cycle
creates tactical rhythm within encounters that sustains tension
across 90-minute battles. Where other campaign games pad their
content with repetitive encounters and filler scenarios, Oathsworn
concentrates its design energy into fewer, deeper experiences. The
result is a game where every session feels significant -- every
battle is memorable, every story choice carries weight, and the
Deepwood's oppressive atmosphere ensures that survival never feels
guaranteed. It is a campaign game built on the principle that less,
done exceptionally well, is more.
